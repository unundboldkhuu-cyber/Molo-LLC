Molo Mine – Khatanbulag sum, Dornogovi province

Molo Mine is in Khatanbulag sum in Dornogovi province, the heart of the Gobi Desert. It is 720 km from Ulaanbaatar and only 150 km away from China's land port Khangi-Mandal. 170 km of paved highway to Khangi-Mandal will be completed 2022 July. 300 km of railroad is planned to be completed in 2025.

Resources & reserves

High‑volume marble resources with strong chemistry

The Molo license hosts two principal deposits, North Ore and Central Ore, with substantial cubic meter resources and favourable calcium oxide and magnesium oxide grades.

Exploration History & License
100.6 ha mining license

The exploration phase in 2017 and 2018 included mapping, testing and licensing which resulted in two main deposits illustrated by the blue area. The mining license covers almost 100.6 hectares of land open for extraction and 931 hectares open for exploration.

The exploration and extraction area is divided into two different deposits: the North Ore and Central Ore. In total, Molo LLC has resources of 41,332,061 cubic meters of resources with a high Calcium Oxide composition. The central ore is a black calcium carbonate marble.
